RepeatProfiler

Jun 2019 - May 2020

RepeatProfiler is a bioinformatics tool that generates, visualizes, and compares repetitive DNA profiles from short-read sequence data and reference sequences. The tool was developed to facilitate the study of repetitive DNA over short evolutionary time scales, especially for groups lacking genomic resources.

This tool was developed in collaboration with another undergraduate and a post-doc fellow in the Larracuente Lab at the University of Rochester.

Clustering Methods for Finding Insights in PRO-CTCAE Data

Jan 2020 - May 2020

I collaborated with 3 other students to conduct exploratory data analysis on a novel dataset (PRO-CTCAE from the URCC13059 study). The PRO-CTCAE collected data on patient-reported outcomes for common terminology criteria for adverse events (symptoms).

EDA methods consisted of correlation, association mining, clustering (k-Means, affinity propagation, spectral clustering, hierarchical clustering, and subclustering), analysis of clusters (metrics, trend tests, etc.), evaluation of clusters on outcome data (physical, mental, hospitalization, and toxicity outcomes of patients), regression, and visualizations of results (PCA, t-SNE, etc.).

Through this project, my teammates and I also had the opportunity to collaborate and discuss our findings with our sponsor team and present our results to the URCC Geriatric-Oncology Research team.

Estimating Recombination Rate in Satellite DNA using Machine Learning Models

Jan 2020 - May 2020

The main objective of this independent research was to investigate whether the copy number distribution of repeats in a simulated population could provide insights about the evolution process, specifically about the recombination rate. I used clustering and regression models to discover where information about the recombination rate could be found in empirical data. This research project was done on populations simulated using the Stephan model of evolution due to the lack of informative real-world data available. My results indicated that, in order to find substantial evidence for a recombination rate, I would need to look beyond copy number distribution.

Analysis of Music Preference Among the UR Schools

Sep 2017 - Dec 2017

I collaborated with 3 other students to collect data concerning the music preferences of students in the different colleges of the University of Rochester. We aggregated, cleaned, and constructed tables and graphs of the collected data using Excel. Together, my group and I analyzed our data using R to conduct Chi-Squared, two-way ANOVA, and Tukey HSD tests. We discovered that students at the Eastman school of music greatly preferred classical music, as expected, and that there were large discrepancies in the amount of money that people would be willing to spend on concerts in different genres. Further data would need to be collected in order the analyze these discrepancies in more detail.
